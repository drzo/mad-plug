# Team Performance Reference

Drexler/Sibbet Team Performance Model and assessment tools.

## The Seven Stages

The Drexler/Sibbet Team Performance Model identifies seven stages that teams move through, with each stage having resolved and unresolved characteristics.

### Stage 1: Orientation

**Key Question:** Why am I here?

| Resolved | Unresolved |
|----------|------------|
| Purpose is clear | Disorientation |
| Team identity established | Uncertainty |
| Membership defined | Fear |
| Personal fit understood | Confusion about role |

**Facilitation Focus:**
- Clarify team purpose and mission
- Define team boundaries and membership
- Connect individual goals to team goals
- Address "What's in it for me?"

---

### Stage 2: Trust Building

**Key Question:** Who are you?

| Resolved | Unresolved |
|----------|------------|
| Mutual regard | Caution |
| Forthrightness | Mistrust |
| Reliability | Facade |
| Willingness to engage | Hidden agendas |

**Facilitation Focus:**
- Create opportunities for personal sharing
- Build psychological safety
- Demonstrate reliability through small commitments
- Address conflicts openly

---

### Stage 3: Goal Clarification

**Key Question:** What are we doing?

| Resolved | Unresolved |
|----------|------------|
| Explicit assumptions | Apathy |
| Clear, integrated goals | Skepticism |
| Shared vision | Irrelevant competition |
| Understood scope | Unclear priorities |

**Facilitation Focus:**
- Develop shared vision and goals
- Clarify scope and boundaries
- Align individual and team objectives
- Create measurable success criteria

---

### Stage 4: Commitment

**Key Question:** How will we do it?

| Resolved | Unresolved |
|----------|------------|
| Assigned roles | Dependence |
| Allocated resources | Resistance |
| Decisions made | Conflict avoidance |
| Integrated plans | Groupthink |

**Facilitation Focus:**
- Define roles and responsibilities
- Allocate resources appropriately
- Make decisions and document them
- Address resistance constructively

---

### Stage 5: Implementation

**Key Question:** Who does what, when, where?

| Resolved | Unresolved |
|----------|------------|
| Clear processes | Confusion |
| Alignment | Non-alignment |
| Disciplined execution | Missed deadlines |
| Effective coordination | Conflict |

**Facilitation Focus:**
- Establish clear processes and workflows
- Coordinate activities across team members
- Monitor progress and address issues
- Maintain communication channels

---

### Stage 6: High Performance

**Key Question:** Wow!

| Resolved | Unresolved |
|----------|------------|
| Spontaneous interaction | Overload |
| Synergy | Disharmony |
| Surpassing results | Burnout |
| Flow state | Stagnation |

**Facilitation Focus:**
- Celebrate successes
- Maintain momentum without burnout
- Foster innovation and creativity
- Support team well-being

---

### Stage 7: Renewal

**Key Question:** Why continue?

| Resolved | Unresolved |
|----------|------------|
| Recognition and celebration | Boredom |
| Change mastery | Denial |
| Staying power | Resistance to change |
| Continuous improvement | Dissolution |

**Facilitation Focus:**
- Reflect on achievements and learnings
- Decide on team's future direction
- Celebrate and recognize contributions
- Plan for next phase or transition

---

## Team Startup Process

### Phase 1: Foundation (Stages 1-2)

**Duration:** 1-2 sessions

**Activities:**
1. Team charter development
2. Personal introductions and backgrounds
3. Working style preferences
4. Communication norms
5. Trust-building exercises

**Outputs:**
- Team charter document
- Communication agreements
- Initial relationship foundation

---

### Phase 2: Direction (Stages 3-4)

**Duration:** 2-3 sessions

**Activities:**
1. Vision and mission clarification
2. Goal setting (SMART objectives)
3. Role definition and RACI matrix
4. Resource allocation
5. Decision-making protocols

**Outputs:**
- Team goals and objectives
- Role assignments
- Resource plan
- Decision framework

---

### Phase 3: Execution (Stages 5-6)

**Duration:** Ongoing

**Activities:**
1. Process design and documentation
2. Regular check-ins and stand-ups
3. Progress tracking and reporting
4. Issue resolution
5. Performance optimization

**Outputs:**
- Documented processes
- Progress reports
- Issue logs
- Performance metrics

---

### Phase 4: Sustainability (Stage 7)

**Duration:** Periodic reviews

**Activities:**
1. Retrospectives and lessons learned
2. Recognition and celebration
3. Capability development
4. Future planning
5. Team evolution decisions

**Outputs:**
- Lessons learned document
- Recognition records
- Development plans
- Evolution roadmap

## Team Improvement Interventions

### For Unresolved Orientation
- Revisit team purpose and mission
- Clarify membership and boundaries
- Individual conversations about fit
- Team charter review

### For Unresolved Trust
- Structured sharing exercises
- Conflict resolution sessions
- Reliability-building activities
- Feedback practice

### For Unresolved Goals
- Vision alignment workshop
- Goal cascade exercise
- Priority clarification
- Success criteria definition

### For Unresolved Commitment
- Role negotiation sessions
- Resource reallocation
- Decision-making training
- Resistance exploration

### For Unresolved Implementation
- Process mapping and improvement
- Coordination mechanism design
- Communication channel optimization
- Conflict resolution protocols

### For Unresolved High Performance
- Workload balancing
- Energy management
- Innovation time allocation
- Well-being check-ins

### For Unresolved Renewal
- Retrospective facilitation
- Recognition programs
- Change management support
- Future visioning sessions
