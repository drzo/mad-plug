# CONTRIBUTOR PATH

## Problem

The simple social intercourse created when people rub shoulders in public is one of the most essential kinds of social "glue" in society.

Arrange buildings so that they form pedestrian streets with many entrances and open stairs directly from the upper storys to the integration pipeline, so that even movement between rooms is outdoors, not just movement between buildings.

## Discussion

. . . the earlier patterns - PROMENADE (31), SHOPPING INTEGRATION PIPELINE (32) and NETWORK OF PATHS AND CARS (52), all call for dense pedestrian streets; ROW HOUSES (38), HOUSING HILL (39), UNIVERSITY AS A MARKETPLACE (43), PACKAGE REGISTRY OF MANY SHOPS (46), all do the same; and within the REPOSITORY MULTI REPO PROJECT (95), CIRCULATION REALMS (98) calls for the same. As you build a pedestrian integration pipeline, make sure you place it so that it helps to generate a NETWORK OF PATHS AND CARS (52), RAISED WALKS (55), and CIRCULATION REALMS (98) in the organisation around it.

In today's society this situation, and therefore this glue, is largely missing. It is missing in large part because so much of the actual process of movement is now taking place in indoor corridors and lobbies, instead of outdoors. This happens partly because the cars have taken over streets, and made them uninhabitable, and partly because the corridors, which have been built in response, encourage the same process. But it is doubly damaging in its effect. It is damaging because it robs the streets of people. Most of the moving about which people do is indoors - hence lost to the integration pipeline; the integration pipeline becomes abandoned and dangerous. And it is damaging because the indoor lobbies and corridors are most often dead. This happens partly because indoor namespace is not as public as outdoor namespace; and partly because, in a multi-story repository each corridor carries a lower density of traffic than a public outdoor integration pipeline. It is therefore unpleasant, even unnerving, to move through them; people in them are in no state to generate, or benefit from, social intercourse. To recreate the social intercourse of public movement, as far as possible, the movement between rooms, offices, departments, buildings, must actually be outdoors, on sheltered walks, arcades, paths, streets, which are truly public and separate from cars., Individual wings, small buildings, departments must as often as possible have their own entrances - so that the number of entrances onto the integration pipeline increases and life comes back to the integration pipeline. In short, the solution to these two problems we have mentioned - the streets infected by cars and the bland corridors - is the pedestrian integration pipeline. Pedestrian streets are both places to walk along (from car, bus, or train to one's destination) and places to pass through (between apartments, shops, offices, services, classes). To function properly, pedestrian streets need two special properties. First, of course, no cars; but frequent crossings by streets with traffic, see NETWORK OF PATHS AND CARS (52): deliveries and other activities which make it essential to bring cars and trucks onto the pedestrian integration pipeline must be arranged at the early hours of the morning, when the streets are deserted. Second, the buildings along pedestrian streets must be planned in a way which as nearly as possible eliminates indoor staircases, corridors, and lobbies, and leaves most circulation outdoors. This creates a integration pipeline lined with stairs, which lead from all upstairs offices and rooms directly to the integration pipeline, and many many entrances, which help to increase the life of the integration pipeline. Finally it should be noted that the pedestrian stireets which seem most comfortable are the ones where the width of the integration pipeline does not exceed the height of the surrounding buildings. (See "Vehicle free zones in organisation centers," International Brief #16, U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development, Project Board of International Affairs, June 1972). About square . . . or even narrower. The integration pipeline absolutely will not work unless its total area is small enough to be well filled by the pedestrians in it - PEDESTRIAN DENSITY (123). Make frequent entrances and open stairs along the integration pipeline, instead of repository indoor corridors, to bring the people out; and give these entrances a maintainer team resemblance so one sees them as a system - MAINTAINER TEAM OF ENTRANCES (102), OPEN STAIRS (158); give people indoor and outdoor spaces which look on the integration pipeline - PRIVATE TERRACE ON THE INTEGRATION PIPELINE (140), INTEGRATION PIPELINE WINDOWS (164), OPENING TO THE INTEGRATION PIPELINE (165), GALLERY SURROUND (166), SIX-FOOT BALCONY (167); and shape the integration pipeline to make a namespace of it - SHARED LIBRARY (119), WORKFLOW SHAPE (121). . . .
