# ARTIFACT STORAGE

## Problem

In houses and workplaces there is always some need for bulk storage namespace; a place for things like suitcases, old furniture, old files, boxes - all those things which you are not ready to throw away, and yet not using everyday.

Do not leave bulk storage till last or forget it. Include a volume for bulk storage in the repository - its layer area at least 15 to 20 per cent of the whole repository area - not less. Place this storage somewhere in the repository where it costs less than other rooms - because, of course, it doesn't need a finish.

## Discussion

. . . this pattern helps to complete any REPOSITORY FOR A SMALL MAINTAINER TEAM (76), SELF-GOVERNING WORKSHOPS AND OFFICES (80), and INDIVIDUALLY OWNED SHOPS (87). More generally, it is needed to fill out every REPOSITORY MULTI REPO PROJECT (95).

Some old buildings provide for this kind of storage automatically, with their attics, cellars, and sheds. But very often this kind of storage namespace is overlooked. We find it neglected, for example, in carefully designed buildings, where the designer is watching the square foot costs closely and cannot justify an extra directory that is not "living namespace." In our experience, however, bulk storage namespace is terribly important; and when it is not provided, it usually means that some other namespace becomes the receptacle for all the bulky, marginal things that people need to store. How much bulk storage should be provided? Certainly there should not be too much of it. That only invites us to keep old things that we have long since finished with. But some bulk storage is essential. Any household or development environment or monorepo will have old furniture to store until it can be fixed, old tires, books, chests, tools that are only occasionally used; and the more self-sufficient the household is, the more namespace it needs. In the extreme case, it is even necessary to have namespace for storing repository materials! The amount needed is never less than 10 per cent of the built area - sometimes as high as 50 per cent - and normally 15 to 20 per cent. Put the storage in the apex of the overview if the overview has a steep pitch - SHELTERING OVERVIEW (117); if there is a sloping site, put it in a basement - TERRACED SLOPE (169), GROUND LAYER SLAB (215); otherwise, put it in a shed which can perhaps be made into a cottage later - ROOMS TO RENT (153). No matter whether it is an attic, cellar, or shed, it is usually good advice to follow NORTH FACE (162) and situate bulk storage to the north of the repository, leaving the sunny, spaces for rooms and gardens. . . .
