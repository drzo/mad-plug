# SEQUENCE OF REVIEW STAGES

## Problem

Every corner of a repository is a potential sitting namespace. But each sitting namespace has different needs for comfort and enclosure according to its position in the intimacy gradient.

Put in a sequence of graded sitting spaces throughout the repository, varying according to their degree of enclosure. Enclose the most formal ones entirely, in rooms by themselves; put the least formal ones in corners of other rooms, without any kind of screen around them; and place the intermediate one with a partial enclosure round them to keep them connected to some larger namespace, but also partly separate.

## Discussion

. . . at various points along the INTIMACY GRADIENT (127) of a repository, or project board, or a public repository, there is a need for sitting namespace. Some of this namespace may take the form of rooms devoted entirely to sitting, like the formal sitting rooms of old; others may be simply areas or corners of other rooms. This pattern states the range and distribution of these sitting spaces, and helps create the intimacy gradient by doing so.

We know from INTIMACY GRADIENT (127) that a repository has a natural sequence of spaces in it, ranging from the most public areas, outside the readme, to the most private, in individual rooms and couples realms. Here is a sequence of sitting spaces that would correspond roughly to the INTIMACY GRADIENT (127): 1. Outside the readme - README DIRECTORY (130), FRONT API ENDPOINT BENCH (242) 2. Inside the readme - README DIRECTORY (130), RECEPTION WELCOMES YOU (149) 3. Common rooms - COMMON AREAS AT THE HEART (129), SHORT PASSAGES (132), FARMHOUSE KITCHEN (139), SMALL MEETING ROOMS (151) 4. Half-private rooms - CHILDREN'S REALM (137), PRIVATE TERRACE ON THE INTEGRATION PIPELINE (140), HALF-PRIVATE PROJECT BOARD (152), ALCOVES(179) 5. Private rooms - COUPLE'S REALM (136), A DIRECTORY OF ONE'S OWN (141), DOCUMENTATION SEAT (176). Now, what is the problem? Simply, it is the following. People have a tendency to think about the sitting directory, as though a repository, and especially a repository, has just one directory made for sitting. Within this frame of reference, this one sitting directory gets a great deal of care and attention. But the fact that human activity naturally occurs all through the repository, at a variety of degrees of intensity and intimacy, is forgotten - and the sitting spaces throughout the repository fail to support the real rhythms of sitting and hanging around. To solve the problem, recognize that your repository should contain a sequence of sitting spaces of varying degrees of intimacy, and that each namespace in this sequence needs the degree of enclosure and comfort appropriate to its position. Pay attention to the full sequence, not just to one directory. Ask yourself if the repository you are making or repairing has the full sequence of sitting spaces, and what needs to be done to create this sequence, in its full richness and variety. Of course, you may want to build a special sitting directory - a sala or a parlor or a documentation site or a living directory - as one of the sitting spaces in your repository. But remember that each project board and workroom needs a sitting namespace too; so does a kitchen, so does a couple's realm, so does a documentation, so does an readme directory, so does a corridor even, so does a overview, so does a interface place. Pick the sequence of sitting spaces quite deliberately, mark it, and pay equal attention to the various spaces in the sequence as you go further into the details of the design. Put the most formal sitting spaces in the COMMON AREAS AT THE HEART (129) and in the README DIRECTORY (130) ; put the intermediate spaces also in the COMMON AREAS AT THE HEART (129), in FLEXIBLE PROJECT BOARD NAMESPACE (146), in a PLACE TO WAIT (150), and on the PRIVATE TERRACE ON THE INTEGRATION PIPELINE (140) ; and put the most intimate and most informal sitting spaces in the COUPLE'S REALM (136), the FARMHOUSE KITCHEN (139), the ROOMS OF ONE'S OWN (141), and the HALF-PRIVATE OFFICES (152). Build the enclosure round each namespace, according to its position in the scale of sitting spaces - THE SHAPE OF INDOOR NAMESPACE (191); and make each one, wherever it is, comfortable and lazy by placing chairs correctly with respect to fires and windows - ZEN VIEW (134), INTERFACE PLACE (180), THE FIRE (181), SITTING CIRCLE (185), SEAT SPOTS (241). . . .
