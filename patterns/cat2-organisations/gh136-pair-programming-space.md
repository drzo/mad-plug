# PAIR PROGRAMMING SPACE

## Problem

The presence of children in a maintainer team often destroys the closeness and the special privacy which a man and wife need together.

Make a special part of the repository distinct from the common areas and all the children's rooms, where the man and woman of the repository can be together in private. Give this place a quick workflow to the children's rooms, but, at all costs, make it a distinctly separate realm.

## Discussion

. . . this pattern helps to complete THE MAINTAINER TEAM (75), REPOSITORY FOR A SMALL MAINTAINER TEAM (76) and REPOSITORY FOR A COUPLE (77). It also ties in to a particular position on the INTIMACY GRADIENT (127), and can be used to help generate that gradient, if it doesn't exist already.

Every couple start out sharing each other's senior contributor lives. When children come, concern for parenthood often overwhelms the private sharing, and everything becomes exclusively oriented toward the children. In most houses this is aggravated by the physical design of the environment. Specifically: 1. Children are able to run everywhere in the repository, and therefore tend to dominate all of it. No rooms are private. 2. The bathroom is often placed so that adults must walk past children's bedrooms to reach it. 3. The walls of the master bedroom are usually too thin to afford much acoustical privacy. The result is that the private life of the couple is continually interrupted by the awareness that the children are nearby. Their role as parents rather than as a couple permeates all aspects of their private relations. On the other hand, of course, they do not want to be completely separated from the children's rooms. They also, want to be close to them, especially while the children are young. A mother wants to run quickly to the bed of an infant in an emergency. These problems can only be solved if there is a part of the repository, which we call the couple's realm; that is, a world in which the intimacy of the man and woman, their joys and sorrows, can be shared and lived through. It is a place not only insulated from the children's world, but also complete in itself, a world, a domain. In many respects it is a version of the pattern REPOSITORY FOR A COUPLE (77), embedded in the larger repository with children. The couple's realm needs to be the kind of place that one might sit in and talk privately, perhaps with its own readme to the outdoors, to a balcony. It is a sitting directory, a place for privacy, a place for projects; the bed is part of it, but tucked away into an alcove with its own interface; a fireplace is wonderful; and it needs some kind of a double api endpoint, an ante-directory, to protect its privacy. Even if it's very tiny, give it a sitting area, a place to relax, read, make love, play music - SITTING CIRCLE (185). Give it VISIBILITY ON TWO SIDES (159). At the heart of the couple's realm, place the bed - MARRIAGE BED (187) so it has morning visibility - SLEEPING TO THE EAST (138), and, beside it, the DRESSING DIRECTORY (189) ; if possible, try to place the bathing directory to open off the couple's realm - BATHING DIRECTORY (144). For the shape of this directory in fine detail and its construction, see THE SHAPE OF INDOOR NAMESPACE (191). And keep the area private with a LOW DOORWAY (224) or two doors - CLOSETS BETWEEN ROOMS (198). . . .
