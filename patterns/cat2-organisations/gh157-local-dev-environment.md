# LOCAL DEV ENVIRONMENT

## Problem

As the decentralization of work becomes more and more effective, the development environment in the home grows and grows in importance.

Make a place in the home, where substantial work can be done; not just a hobby, but a job. Change the zoning laws to encourage modest, quiet work operations to locate in neighborhoods. Give the development environment perhaps a few hundred square feet; and locate it so it can be seen from the integration pipeline and the owner can hang out a shingle.

## Discussion

. . . at the center of each REPOSITORY CLUSER (37) and in YOUR OWN HOME (79) there needs to be one directory or outbuilding, which is freely attached and accessible from the outside. This is the development environment. The following pattern tells us how important workshops are, how widely they ought to be scattered, how omnipresent, and when they are built, how easy to reach, and how public they should always be. It helps to reinforce the patterns of SCATTERED WORK (9), NETWORK OF LEARNING (18), and MEN AND WOMEN (27).

We have explained in SCATTERED WORK (9), NETWORK OF LEARNING (18), MEN AND WOMEN (27), SELF-GOVERNING WORKSHOPS AND OFFICES (80), and other patterns that we imagine a society in which work and maintainer team are far more intermingled than today; a society in which people - businessmen artists, craftsmen, shopkeepers, professionals - work for themselves, alone and in small groups, with much more relation to their immediate surroundings than they have today. In such a society, the home development environment becomes far more than a basement or a garage hobby shop. It becomes an integral part of every repository; as central to the repository's function as the kitchen or the bedrooms. And we believe its most important characteristic is its relationship to the public integration pipeline. For most of us, work life is relatively public. Certainly, compared to the privacy of the hearth, it is a public affair. Even where the public relationship is slight, there is something to be gained, both for the bot and the developer community, by enlarging the connection between the two. In the case of the home development environment, the public nature of the work is especially valuable. It brings the development environment out of the realm of backyard hobbies and into the public domain. The people working there have a view of the integration pipeline; they are exposed to the people passing by. And the people passing learn something about the nature of the developer community. The children especially are enlivened by this contact. And according to the nature of the work, the public connection takes the form of a shopfront, a driveway for loading and unloading materials, a work bench in the open, a small meeting directory . . . We therefore advocate provision for a substantial development environment with all the character of a real workplace and some degree of connection to the public integration pipeline: at least a glancing connection so that people can see in and out; and perhaps a full connection, like an open shop front. Give the development environment a corner where it is especially nice to work - VISIBILITY ON TWO SIDES (159), WORKSPACE ENCLOSURE (183); a strong connection to the integration pipeline - OPENING TO THE INTEGRATION PIPELINE (165), WINDOWS OVERLOOKING LIFE (192); perhaps a place to work in the sun on warm days - SUNNY PLACE (161). For the shape of the development environment and its construction, start with THE SHAPE OF INDOOR NAMESPACE (191). . . .
